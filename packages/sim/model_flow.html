params — lap / deg / DNF / start
joint fuel+tyre model, deg cliff, compound offsets, circuit profiles
prime suspect

Fits a within-race fixed-effects model for fuel & per-compound degradation, imposes a cliff past a data-driven knee, and regularises fresh-tyre compound offsets toward the known SOFT<MEDIUM<HARD order. Hierarchically shrunk (circuit→global), recency-weighted. Feeds the sim and the analytic economics in generation.

BREAK #1 — label-keyed deg vs 2026 nomination shift. Deg/offsets are keyed to the relative label (SOFT/MEDIUM/HARD), fit on 2021–25. When Pirelli nominates a step softer (Barcelona 2026), the label→physical mapping is wrong: the sim rates the wrong tyres fast.
Evidence: Barcelona 2026 recall 100% but set@k 12% — the winning MEDIUM-HARD-HARD is generated but rated ~20s slow.
BREAK #2 — soft deg slope over-estimated. A soft stint's modelled deg is too steep, so soft strategies look slow even when they win.
Evidence: Mexico 2025 — SOFT-MEDIUM won from pole, sim rates it E[finish]≈8. This poisons the competitiveness signal (see stage 5).

3
generation — candidates + prior + shortlist
every rule-legal strategy, pit windows, plausibility prior, family shortlist
order-blind

Enumerates all rule-legal compound sequences (≥2 compounds, allocation caps), sets pit windows from tyre economics blended toward observed history, and attaches a hierarchical plausibility prior. shortlist() keeps the best variant of every family = (n_stops, compound multiset).

Plausibility prior

prior(seq) = P(stops) · P(pattern) · P(start compound) · P(weekend usage) — circuit evidence shrunk toward a recency-weighted global meta.

BREAK #3 — the prior is ORDER-BLIND. P(pattern) is over the multiset and P(start) only sees the first compound — nothing distinguishes MEDIUM-MEDIUM-HARD from MEDIUM-HARD-MEDIUM. So the shortlist collapses each family to one order and the engine has no signal to rank orderings.
Evidence: this caps order@k. The naive "5 most common historical sequences" baseline (44%) beats the model (36%) on 2025 order.
Ceiling: generation recall is 90.5% (2025) / 97.1% (2026) — set@k can never exceed this. Recall is not the main problem.

Prime suspects — where to dig

1Pace model is label-keyed

stage 2 · params/lapmodel.py

2026 tyres a step softer breaks the label→physical map. Fix is nomination-aware: ingest Pirelli C-numbers, or weight 2026 practice long-runs far harder. Biggest single 2026 lever (Barcelona 12→~80 set@k).

2Prior is order-blind

stage 3 · generation/plausibility.py

Track full-sequence frequencies (not just multiset + start) so the pool carries and ranks the right ordering. This is the only real order@k lever — currently order@k < the trivial baseline on 2025.

3Competitiveness signal is unreliable

stages 4–5 · sim + evaluation

The sim barely separates strategies and mis-rates soft ones — so tuning discards it for selection. Fixing pace (suspect 1) + fitting the sim's overtaking/SC params would make comp usable, unlocking the ~12–21% selector-skip band.

4Fixed 5-slot budget vs field diversity

stage 6 · structural

Fields run ~4.7 distinct sets/race; the oracle ceiling is ~97% set / ~90% order. Real, but not the current bottleneck — suspects 1–3 come first.
